Style Guide

Goal and Aesthetic

My goal was to show the visual progress of the internet, my aesthetic across the website was tied into this i.e. either old aesthetic or new.

Visual Design Elements

Line – the webpages are filled with straight lines, whether visible (nav bar) or abstract (between text). This gives the page structure, and keeps it neat and orderly looking; which is very important for my modern (minimalistic) aesthetic.

Shape – In keeping with this minimalistic aesthetic, the shapes are sharp and blocky. These pristine edges are very closely related to my aesthetic. The font as well is blocky, but without being sharp and harsh. Instead it appears bold.

The only exception to the square shapes, is the buttons in the control panel, which were styled to look like buttons on a remote control.

Space – My information, and points of interest are laid out in the center of the page. This (usually) leaves some negative space on either side, which guides the user along the center of the page. Again, the exception lies with the control panel, which is placed on the right, as most users will be used to focusing their vision on the left (with game menus and even some websites). This leaves them free to play with the remote, but focus on the content.

Color – The yellow color is strong and bright, intensely contrasting with the vivid black. The juxtaposition between light and dark is very visually striking, and appealing (I hope).

Texture – there is a single example of texture on this website, and once again it lies in the exception of the (exceptional) control panel. The buttons are styled to look and feel like a button. When interacted with, the effect coupled with the tapping of a track pad, or clicking of a mouse button is very satisfying.

Justification of Significant Design Decisions (Features Not Bugs)

I will go through these starting from the top of the page.

There is a space above the navigation bar. This is intentional as I enjoy the visual appeal of it. It gives the nav bar that stripey look/feel which I believe adds the to the modern aesthetic.

Sometimes the links might seem difficult to see against the background. Before fixing this, I looked at the effect that this had. When looking at a link that was difficult to see against the background (white on yellow) I realized that the hover effect became useful, not only in highlighting the link, but effectively filling up what could previously be assumed to be negative space (with subtle hints to suggest otherwise). This zoned me into that link.

In the blogs, I have really tightened the content into the center of the page, to make reading the blogs easier. The negative space controls the vision I guess.
