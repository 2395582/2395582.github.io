Styleguide

The first data-art on this site is a simple word cloud. I already go indepth on the deign of it in my reflection (available on the blog page).

The second data-art, for the exam, is based off an example I found on D3.JS. It uses images to great effect to provide the user with the optimised visual rpresentation of the data set... or at least as close as I can get without pirating the movie and hosting it on my site.

The exam data-art follows a simple design. It is placed in the centre of the screen, using negative space to highlight its signifigance. It appears in the form of a film strip, a homage to the filmstrip decoration I've mentioned in previous iterations.

This film strip method, I think, works to great effect. Each 'frame' in the strip can be more closely investigated, and by clicking on it, even more will be revealed. I believe this is a fun way of incorporating interaction in the data-art.

As for the matter of incorporating 'art' into the data-art, I believe I achieved this. The artwork is appealing as it incorporates the most exciting bit of the data set, the actual movies. The filmstrip notion adds to the visual appeal, and the zoom effect does the same.

It also engages with the previous data-art, by enhancing it. It continues the notion of rank ordering the movies, but gives much more information than just a movie title.
