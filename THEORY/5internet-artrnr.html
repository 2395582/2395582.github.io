THRIVE

Internet-Art - Rationale & Reflection

Description

My internet art is based off the advancement in terms of style that internet and more specifically its webpages undertook. The artwork starts off as a normal, barebones HTML page, mirroring the first ever webpage. The user than has the opportunity to use the provided to buttons, to change various aspects of the page such as color and font.

Rationale

By clicking on the buttons, the user is effectively skipping years of internet evolution with a simple click of a button. The button clicks show the relation as well as the transformation between the first ever webpage, and the modern-day internet. I have linked the notorious ‘first ever webpage’ to guide the user in the same direction that I was when I first got this idea. It’s a very “That’s the first webpage!” line of thought, which I believe (hope) will spark some introspection in the user. The internet is just another tool for us today, but to only view it as such, is to disregard the artistic elements of it. When I watched the first lecture, which I believe was titled “net/web art”, my brain kept autocorrecting it to “web/art”. Now maybe this is a bit of a stretch, but web/art makes a lot of sense. The web is (not always, but in more ways than we sometimes realize) art.

Critical Reflection

This is the part where I rip myself to shreds before you can. Dracula told Igor: “Do unto others before they do unto you” (from the crappy, so bad its good movie Van Helsing) but I guess in this case its “Do unto you before others do.” Let’s begin.

Let’s begin with the good. The artwork, I believe shows the core message that I tried to portray. Of the few people that I showed it to, all had the same reaction, (with varying degrees of interest – my dad's more of a newspaper guy) of “That’s what it looked like?” I believe in that regard the artwork is effective in eliciting that response, and perhaps sparking that introspection I spoke of earlier.

However, I do realize, that with an idea like this I could do a lot more aesthetically speaking. My only drawbacks were technical prowess, and time. However, as the brief states a “small scale Internet Artwork” I believe this artwork is still effective. The changes that the buttons bring to the website, while not the flashiest, are flashy enough by comparison that the effect is achieved. Granted, it only takes a bit of CSS tweaking to be flashier than an HTML doc, but that’s the whole point.
