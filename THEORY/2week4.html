THRIVE

WEEK 4 - DEV BLOG

APIs posed quite a problem to me last year, and I look forward to getting more practice so I can reach a point where the mention of APIs do not induce a deep and long-lasting trauma. Wish me luck.

In Mailloux’s (?) paper on convention he states an interesting notion which grabbed me. The unconventional, if accepted by the community, becomes convention. (First they hate, then they imitate). There is no denying that anything against convention in any practice would first be questioned, and/or scorned, but it also has the potential to inculcate itself into that practice. But before we can find out if something will become convention, we are shocked by it. And if its got staying power…

This I believe leaves two paths for artists and designers, or indeed any hoping to add in some way to a craft. The conventional path. Its safe, tried and tested. You know how it works, your audience knows how it works, and its easy to keep everyone on the same page. At first glance it may seem… too safe. Boring perhaps. Risk free and thus minimal rewards. But in some contexts this is exactly what you want. In some contexts; if we don’t do things the way they supposed to be done, how can we expect things to come out the way we want.

Why do we progress when we shouldn’t? Because its sometimes a chance that has to be taken. Sometimes we must step out of the circle the fire creates and venture into the dark unknown. Some would say, this is exactly what an artist does. Step into the unknown and bring out some treasures for the rest of the people. The people would, naturally, react with shock to unknown, but if what the artist has said has staying power, it will remain with them indefinitely. Like Bill Withers said/sang; “The words that say the most, say things that last.”

What I took from this (besides the ramblings seen above) was that in this task there is an element of both these paths. On a technical level when, when creating web art, one would do well to follow conventions in the construction of the website. HTML never changes, CSS always looks like CSS and JavaScript is a bitch. No real room for shocks.

Until you get to the art…
