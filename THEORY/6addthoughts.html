Additional Thoughts

“There seem to be almost no rules, except that the artwork be of the internet.”

This is an interesting caveat for an artwork. Every art form, and subsequently every work of art, is limited in a similar way. Movies are shot on a camera. Books are written. In order to categorize an artwork, the rule is that it must be dependent on certain tools to be made. To be made in any way the artist sees fit yes, but made with those tools.

Internet art, has to be dependent on the internet. Definitions for internet art can be hard to come by, so as a rule I apply the statement “Would this be possible if the internet never existed?”. Not a very hard and fast rule, but that’s the point I’m getting to. (Slowly but surely)

The internet is a World Wide Wonderland with almost infinite possibilities. As full of potential as a blank page.

The internet is also the tool that must be used to create Internet Art. (See where I’m going with this?)

What happens when the tool used in the creation of art, is able to do almost everything? Well, you should know since you’ve been marking my peer’s work for the past few minutes/hours/days. (If it's days, do take a rest, even a smoke break will do)My answer is: You’ll get art that realizes as much potential as the artist wanted to realize for their needs. The internet can do anything, but all it needs to do is what I want it to do.

This is why, I think, all the internet art I perused in my research were so confusing to me. The limitless potential lead to limitless creation and the results were very difficult to group together. While at the time, I admit I wrote it off as artsy BS, I believe I’ve followed a line of logic that has allowed me to reason with it. (Yes, because that's what art needs, logic and reason.)

Internet Art is uncategorizable , because the internet is uncategorizable. An extreme statement I know, lacking some nuance, but it’s a fitting addition to my musings. Extreme, and lacking some nuance.
