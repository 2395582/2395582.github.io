THRIVE

Internet-Art - Preparation

The preparation of this art project began firstly, with a familiarization of Internet Art as an artform. I perused the various examples with as open a mind as I could muster, (which is pretty open considering the degree I’ve dedicated almost four years to now) and… Internet Art is interesting. There seem to be almost no rules, except that the artwork be of the internet.

An artwork created using the internet as a tool that is. In this digital craft, the internet is to the artwork, what a paint brush is to a painting. Or a chisel to a sculptor. Or Robert De Niro to Martin Scorsese. The artwork has to be dependent on the internet.

All my musings in these early stages revolved, naturally around the internet. It was a tool I used every day, but never to make art. As I continued to think about the internet, browsing both for business and pleasure, simultaneously doing research on internet art, as well as checking out the style used on websites, a thought occurred to me. Not exactly a “Eureka!” moment, but the beginnings of one.

Where did the Internet start? And more importantly for my purposes, how did look it when it started? That line of thought quickly lead me to The Project which is the genesis of the World Wide Web as we know it. And there it was. In black and white. And blue. (Great color palate, right?) I found it so strange to think, that over the years we’ve gone from that to the websites we see today. Just a little bit of style. A little bit of style that went a long way.

And that is how I came to the decision of creating a website that celebrated that progression. “Progress” was actually the original name of this website. But, in keeping with my attempts to have a modern aesthetic, I decided on “THRIVE”. All caps and bold. Just the type of word you’d see slapped on to a mass-produced T-Shirt and sold in clothing stores the world over. (Next to a T-shirt that says “SLAY” which I think is a dragons and dungeons reference). “THRIVE.” Very modern. Hip, I should say? (No, no probably not)

As for the more practical preparation, I browsed the websites from previous years, for inspiration. I quite like the idea here Iman's Corner where the user was given control over the artwork. User-controlled works of art are a common theme in this, my game design degree, so the idea really grabbed my attention. Following this line of thought, is what got me to where I am now.

The technical work that went into this was fairly simple. As per the brief I relied heavily on HTML and CSS, only using JS where necessary. In order for the artwork to work, I had to find various example of different color combinations which I could use. I found them here Digital Synopsis

All in all, I think the artwork, though deceptively simple worked. But more on that in the reflection.
