Dependancy Blog

For the inclusion of a Javascript Dependancy/API or Library, I wanted one that would enhance the feel of my portfolio. As such, I used one which made my portfolio pieces more readable.

The Dependancy in question uses Javascript to create a page flip animation, when a button is clicked. This, I believe, creates the feel of 'flipping through a portfolio', which I thought would be a fun and visually interesting way to do it.

It also compiles all my portfolio pieces in one, easy to navigate page, where the pieces can be read one after the other, or the menu can be accessed to peruse any piece whose title strikes your fancy.

I believe a page flip Dependancy makes the most sense given the context of my portfolio, which is writing. In terms of alternatives, I considered a Dependancy which would allow me to add a search bar, to make navigation more streamlined, but ultimately decided on the page flip.

Asides from the visual appeal, I believe the Dependancy also aids in making the perusal of the portfolio (one of the core tenets of the website) easier. Because, as I have mentioned, it compiles all my works in one. The menu function is particularly helpful in that readers can skip to whichever story they want, without needing to flip through the whole document. The menu would be especially helpful in the future, if more pieces are added to the portfolio. Multiple works would be compiled in one document, and several similarly styled documents could be used to give works from different stages in my 'career.'

It is not, however, without some flaws. As the Dependancy is opened on a new tab, the user has to navigate back by changing tabs. I attempted several methods of streamlining this, but was unsuccesful. However, I do believe, opening it in a new tab makes it somewhat easier in a different regard. Readers interested solely in the portfolio, can access the website, get the document, then close all other tabs and peruse to their hearts content.
