Right-Clicks and Responsibilites

"Whether internet access is a human right or whether it should simply be included in the category of media that facilitate the exercise of human rights is a philosophical debate. " - Kasapis 2020

A philosophical debate I am quite interested in.

Internet access as a human right, would call for a much wider access to the Internet, across the globe. This, from all points of view, makes sense. It is basically a call for an equal distribution of knowledge, as the Internet at its very core is a system of information, and information is knowledge.

Therefore, as I have mentioned, from all points of view, this equality is desirable. Even the most selfish amongst us, could understand, that this globally equal distribution of knowledge, would, by extension, lead to a dramatic increase in the realization of potential across the globe. The more potential is realized, the more society, and hence the individual (even the most self-serving one) would stand to gain.

Naturally, efforts must be made, to make this a reality. The question of whether or not they are being made, is a tough one. But, ideally speaking, I do agree that access to the internet is, or should be, a human right. It is, in essence, the right to education, in perhaps the least formal sense of the word, but possibly also the least limited.

As for the question of Internet as a “media that facilitates the exercise of human rights”. The first, basic human right that comes to mind, in this context, is freedom of speech. Naturally, as the Internet, or at least a huge part of the Internet, is filled with social interactions.

To see these interactions as an extension of the real-life interactions we have is a matter of course. Just like these live interactions (mostly) facilitate the exercise of human rights, such as freedom of speech, the extensions of these interactions should do the same. In a time where talking to a person face to face, is barely any different with talking to someone in a different time zone, these basic human rights should continue to prevail.
