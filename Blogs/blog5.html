USE ME - Bill Withers

The UI/UX on my website in assignment 1 was… a bit problematic. There were certain decisions I made which worked. Some did not.

In terms of decisions that worked, first and foremost for me, was the recurring them throughout the website. In keeping with the portfolio type, writing, the website was made to look like a library. The background on each page reenforced the idea of the page, books on a shelf for a collection of stories, blueprints for the design section etc.. There was also the constant reminder of which page the user is on, in the form of a title. This coupled with the constant navigation bar made perusing the four main pages a relatively easy process. On each page there is a introduction to what to expect from it, given in short concise sentences, in the hope that once the user gets instant understanding of what to expect they can start perusing to their hearts delight.

I also chose a mild, warm color palate which would give the… vibe of a warm and cozy library. Aesthetically, I believe this worked.

As for what did not work, it was the actual posts on the website. Although the blogs and portfolio pieces retained the warm color, they lost points in terms of being difficult to read and navigation away from said posts was made difficult by the unfortunate exclusion of a navigation bar. The biggest downfall, is clearly the text appearing as huge, daunting, screen-end, to screen-end, walls of text… not a good idea for a writing portfolio website.

I intend to fix this formatting and also add a footer on the posts to facilitate easier movement.
